Bank Financing Option

Specialist RIA Lenders — A Fourth Financing Path

Specialist banks — Live Oak Bank, Oak Street Funding, SkyView Partners, and PPC Loan — actively finance internal RIA partner buyouts at Salvus's size. The loan goes to the buyer (O'Quinn, then Pete, then buyers of Bob's stake), not to Salvus. This is the most important structural insight: Chuck and Bob can receive cash at closing instead of holding a seller note.

Critical finding — SBA is incompatible with Salvus's tranched succession design. SBA 7(a) rules prohibit partial equity purchases and tranched succession. The Recommendation

Summary of the recommended financing approach: Bank financing from a specialist RIA lender is the primary recommended structure — it gives Chuck cash at closing rather than a deferred note. The seller note is retained only as a small subordinated piece (typically required by the lender as a confidence signal). The pure seller-note approach remains available as a fallback but is no longer the first choice.
Salvus Legacy 24 · Updated Recommendation · For Discussion with Counsel

Blended Valuation · Bank Financing Primary · Small Seller Note Subordinated · Orderly 2027–2029 Exit

  • Valuation formula — unchanged: Average of (a) 6x TTM net advisory revenue after 7% TPG platform fee and (b) 9x TTM Net Ordinary Income, less 15% illiquidity discount and 5% no-auction discount. At April 2027 projections: approximately $4.1M for Chuck's full 33.3% SL24 interest. This formula is fair, defensible, and consistent with 2025–2026 RIA industry data.
  • Primary financing — conventional bank loan (Oak Street Funding or PPC Loan): O'Quinn (and Bob via ROFR) borrow approximately 80% of each tranche price from a specialist RIA lender. Chuck receives cash at each tranche closing — no waiting, no performance risk. Important: SBA 7(a) loans are incompatible with Salvus's tranched structure — use conventional financing only. Oak Street Funding and PPC Loan are both designed specifically for multi-year internal equity transitions like this one.
  • Down payment from TPG proceeds: The $2,080,000 from the additional 10% TPG revenue sale in 2027 provides the down payment for the first large tranche — or eliminates the need for one entirely, depending on the lender's requirements. This was always the plan; bank financing does not change it.
  • Subordinated seller note (~20%): A small seller note of approximately $820K (20% of the $4.1M buyout) is retained — not because it is preferred, but because many conventional lenders require seller participation as a signal of confidence in the firm's continued success. This note is subordinated to the bank loan, carries a 5.5% rate, and is paid over 6 years (~$157K/yr). Chuck still receives 80%+ of his proceeds as cash at closing.
  • What this means for Chuck: Under the pure seller note plan, Chuck received $2.08M at close and then waited 6 years for the remaining $2.0M. Under the recommended bank financing plan, Chuck receives approximately $3.3M at the 2027 close (bank portion) plus the $820K seller note paid over 6 years. He has collected more than 80% of his total proceeds within 12 months of the first tranche — a dramatically better outcome with far less retirement risk.
  • What this means for Bob and O'Quinn (buyers): The annual obligation is approximately $564K/yr (bank ~$407K + note ~$157K) versus $393K/yr on the pure seller note. The extra ~$171K/yr reflects the higher bank rate (9% vs. 5.5%). This is covered by their growing combined K-1 income — at 2027 combined NOI of $2M+, both buyers can service the debt comfortably. The cost is real but the tradeoff — Chuck exits cleanly — is worth it.
  • Bob's first right of refusal on all tranches at the formula price. Chuck may sell only to current or future SL24 members. TPG/Purple Marlin is permanently excluded from SL24. The SL24 Operating Agreement must explicitly permit equity purchases financed by third-party conventional lenders, including any lender consent-to-transfer provisions required by the bank.
  • This same blended formula and bank financing approach becomes the standard for all future SL24 transitions — including Pete's admission and Bob's eventual exit — creating consistency, predictability, and fairness for all future partners.
