How Net Ordinary Income flows to each partner · K-1 (incl. ROQ book share 2027+) + seller note obligations
SWM Comp: Gross Revenue → minus TPG % fee → minus operating expenses → Net Ordinary Income → distributed per SL24 ownership % as K-1 income to all partners.
O'Quinn Pre-Equity (2026 catch-up only): June 2026 one-off billing ~$175K net split 60/20/20 — ROQ 60%, Bob 20%, Chuck 20%. This is the last compensation paid under the old structure.
O'Quinn Post-Equity (2027+ as SL24 partner): Once admitted as equity partner in April 2027, O'Quinn's compensation works exactly like Bob's and Chuck's — he receives his SL24 ownership % share of combined Net Ordinary Income as K-1 income. His book revenue flows through ThePARTNERS into the combined NOI pool, then splits per SL24 %. Bob and Chuck receive NO separate override on his book — their additional benefit comes through their own larger K-1 share of the now-bigger combined NOI.

Bank Financing — Specialist RIA Lenders
Live Oak Bank · Oak Street Funding · SkyView Partners · PPC Loan · Comparison vs. seller note · Adjust Bank Loan Rate and Bank % of Buyout sliders above
The key insight: Specialist RIA lenders finance internal partner buyouts — O'Quinn and Pete borrow from the bank, Chuck and Bob receive cash at closing. The loan is to the BUYER, not to Salvus. This eliminates the seller note's retirement risk entirely. Critical caveat: SBA 7(a) loans cannot fund tranched/partial buyouts — Salvus must use conventional financing (Oak Street, PPC, SkyView) for its multi-tranche structure.
