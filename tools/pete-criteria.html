Section 01

Why This Framework Matters

Admitting a new equity partner is one of the most consequential decisions a firm can make. Done right, it accelerates growth, deepens client retention, and builds the next generation of leadership. Section 07

The Recommendation

Internal Planning · Bob Joel · June 2026

A Clear, Objective, Measurable Path — No Promises Until Criteria Are Met

  • Do not discuss equity with Pete at this stage. The framework exists for internal planning. Any premature conversation creates expectations that are difficult to walk back — and may not be appropriate if Pete does not meet the criteria. Let performance drive the conversation.
  • Begin annual scorecard tracking in 2027. Document Pete's revenue, relationships, originations, and firm contributions each year — even informally. This creates an objective record and protects Salvus in any future dispute or misunderstanding.
  • Set performance expectations with Pete explicitly — without mentioning equity. Tell Pete what you need from him in terms of revenue growth, client ownership, and origination. This gives him every opportunity to earn partnership without creating a binding commitment.
  • Target the Standard Path (2029 admission) as the base case. If Pete accelerates — pull forward. If he stalls — defer without guilt. The scorecard is the arbiter, not tenure, relationship, or sentiment.
  • This same framework applies to any future IAR considering partnership — creating a consistent, fair, and legally defensible process for all future equity admissions at Salvus Legacy 24 LLC. Consistency matters as much as the criteria themselves.
